# HARROWGATE KEEP

*An old-school exploration module for AD&D 1e / OSRIC. Region: the eastern march of Halvedd, the Known World (Orruvane), on the ridge above the Harrow ford. Nearest town: Wend (walled caravan town; inn: the Broken Wheel), about a day-and-a-half west. Recommended: 1-3 characters of level 4 (or a party of 4-6 of levels 2-4).*

## PREMISE
Harrowgate was a royal toll-and-watch keep guarding the Harrow ford and the east road on the old march border — a garrison of thirty, a portcullis, a grain-store, a castellan's crypt. About sixty years ago a treaty redrew the border; the keep lost its purpose and its pay, the garrison marched out (sealing the last toll-take in the gatehouse wall), and the place was left to the weather. A spring flood later undercut and breached the river-wall, drowning the lower cistern and undercroft. Squatters came and went. Then a hobgoblin warband moved in to raid the road — and found they could not hold the wet cellars, because the flood-dead would not stay drowned. Today a fang of hobgoblins holds the surface, a handful of ghouls hunt the flooded undercroft, and a Wend merchant rots in the gatehouse cell over a ransom that never came.

## THE GROUPS

**The Fang (hobgoblins).** *[CAMPAIGN STATE: destroyed/fled — kept here for the authored record.]* A disciplined warband, ~14 hobgoblins + 3 elite guards under **Grorm**, a scarred sub-chief. They drilled, posted sentries, flew a banner, and raided the east road and outlying steadings, taking captives for ransom. They held the surface but **would not go below the waterline** — they had lost warriors to the "drowned dead" and were badly spooked. Goal: dominate the road, grow the fang. Grorm: AC 4 (mail), HD 3, **hp 16**, two-handed sword 1d10, +1 to hit (strong). Elite guards (3): AC 4, HD 2, **hp 11, 9, 12**, longsword 1d8. Hobgoblins (14): AC 5, HD 1+1, **hp 5,7,3,6,4,8,5,2,6,7,4,5,3,6**, longsword/morningstar 1d8, morale 8 (9 near Grorm).

**The Drowned Dead (ghouls).** Five ghouls denning in the flooded crypt and undercroft (11/12) — the flood- and war-dead, risen; no one knows why and the module does not say. They hunt the wet dark and ignore the dry surface; the hobgoblins feared them. **Ghouls (5):** AC 6, HD 2, **hp 5, 13, 4, 7, 15**, atk claw/claw/bite 1d3/1d3/1d6 + paralysis (save or held; *elves immune*), move 90 ft, morale 60. Holy water harms them.

**Oren Pelk (captive).** *[CAMPAIGN STATE: rescued, back in Wend, now Sylas's ally/fence.]* A Wend wool-merchant taken on the road, penned with two servants (one feverish) for a ransom his factor never paid (a lost message, a frightened factor). Non-combatant; rumor- and reward-source. Freed, he pays well and opens the wool-road.

## ROOM KEY

### 1. GATEHOUSE & BARBICAN
**Was:** the keep's only formal entry — a portcullis, murder-holes, a guard-passage, and a small strongroom where the toll-silver was counted.
**Now:** *[looted]* the fang's gate-post. The rusty portcullis still works (down, not flush). In a guard-passage alcove the old garrison **bricked up the last toll-take** when they marched out: behind the loose-mortared wall, an iron strongbox — **900 gp, 300 sp, 2 gems (250 gp ea), the castellan's silver seal-matrix (60 gp), and the old toll-rolls** (worthless coin; useful local history). Found on a search of the odd brickwork.

### 2. BAILEY
**Was:** the drill-yard and heart of the keep — a well, the banner-pole, daily muster.
**Now:** *[garrisoned by Sylas's Harrowgate Six]* packed dirt, cook-fire scars, the banner-pole (banner taken). The well gives good water. Open ground, overlooked from the wall-walk.

### 3. GREAT HALL / BARRACKS (keep ground)
**Was:** the garrison's hall — hearth, weapon-racks, trestle tables, where the soldiers ate and slept.
**Now:** *[cleared/looted]* the fang's common quarters: trophy wall, a kept hearth, bedding. Held the bulk of the hobgoblins off-watch.

### 4. CASTELLAN'S CHAMBER (keep upper)
**Was:** the commander's room over the hall — bed, chest, the keep's records.
**Now:** *[looted — Grorm dead]* Grorm's quarters: furs, the war-banner, and his **warchest: 1,200 gp, 600 sp, 4 gems (500, 250, 100, 100), an (unidentified) Potion of Healing, and a Magic-User scroll (Web, Mirror Image)** — all taken in raids.

### 5. KITCHENS & STORES (keep, behind hall)
**Was:** the keep's kitchen and dry-store.
**Now:** cold ovens, a cook-crew's mess; a back stair descends to the cellars (10).

### 6. CAPTIVES' CELL (old guardroom, barred)
**Was:** a guardroom by the gate.
**Now:** *[empty — Oren freed]* a barred cell; held Oren Pelk and two servants for ransom. One hobgoblin warder kept the key.

### 7. STABLES & FORGE
**Was:** patrol-horse stables and a farrier's forge.
**Now:** *[horses taken]* a lean-to with a captured smith's tools (low value).

### 8. SHRINE
**Was:** a small soldiers' shrine to a soldier-saint, where the garrison prayed before patrols; the floor-stair leads to the castellan's crypt.
**Now:** stripped altar, a faded carving of an armored figure with a reversed sword, dead candle-stubs. The hobgoblins shunned it (it goes down to the dead). Behind the altar-carving, a hidden niche holds **40 gp in old silver coin** (search to find). Worn stone stair DOWN to the crypt (12).

### 9. WALL-WALK & TOWERS
**Was:** the fighting walk and four corner towers.
**Now:** *[sentries fled]* the NE tower is flood-cracked into a **climbable rubble breach** (an alternate way in).

### 10. CELLARS & CISTERN-HEAD (undercroft)
**Was:** the keep's vaulted dry-store and the head of its cistern.
**Now:** partly flooded; mead casks and **honey crocks worth ~120 gp in bulk** (needs the cart to haul). The flood-breach (15) lets river and intruders in; giant rats infest the damp.
> **Giant Rats (6):** AC 7, HD 1d4, **hp 4, 4, 1, 2, 2, 1**, bite 1d3 (5% disease), move 120 ft, morale 50. *(ids 37-42)*

### 11. FLOODED UNDERCROFT
**Was:** lower store-rooms and the cistern body.
**Now:** knee-to-waist black water, floating debris, drowned rooms. The crypt ghouls hunt here; no separate creatures. A wader risks the cold and the dark.

### 12. CASTELLAN'S CRYPT
**Was:** the burial vault of the keep's castellans — niches, an ossuary, the founder's stone bier.
**Now:** ankle-deep water weeping in from a slumped passage; burial niches; the **5 ghouls** den here. On the founder's bier, the stone effigy of the first castellan; *beneath* it, interred with him, the keep's old treasure: **WARDFFAN, a +1 longsword** (plain soldier's steel, faintly warm to an honest grip) and grave-silver — **a silver torc (200 gp) and two silver arm-rings (75 gp ea).** Holy water bites the ghouls; an elf ignores their paralysis.
> **Ghouls (5):** AC 6, HD 2, **hp 5, 13, 4, 7, 15**, claw/claw/bite 1d3/1d3/1d6 + paralysis, morale 60. *(ids 24-28)*

### 13. SALLY-PORT / CISTERN DRAIN
**Was:** a defended water-gate and drain to the river, for sorties and fresh water.
**Now:** a slimed tunnel with a short submerged stretch, opening on the riverbank — an alternate entry/escape.

### 14. GRANARY TOWER (freestanding, in the bailey)
**Was:** the keep's grain-store tower.
**Now:** *[cache looted]* rotted grain, deep chaff; a deserter once hid a stash under a flagstone (150 gp + a Potion of Climbing — now taken). Giant rats nest in the husks.
> **Giant Rats (8):** AC 7, HD 1d4, **hp 1, 1, 4, 3, 2, 1, 2, 4**, bite 1d3 (5% disease), morale 50. *(ids 29-36)*

### 15. RIVER-WALL BREACH (exterior, riverside)
**Was:** sound curtain wall above the water.
**Now:** a flood-broken gap of tumbled masonry, half in the current — wade or climb into the cellars (10) / flooded undercroft (11), bypassing the gate entirely. Unwatched.

## THE ONE STRANGE THING *(do not explain)*
**The weeping effigy (room 12).** The stone knight atop the founder's bier beads a slow, rust-red water from its carved eyes; the little puddle in the effigy's lap never dries, even in the dry months, and some nights the whole crypt smells of hot iron and woodsmoke though nothing burns. The ghouls den all through the crypt but never foul or touch the effigy itself. Whether it is mineral seep, the founder's grief, a warning, or nothing at all, the module does not know and neither should the referee. Present it; let the table decide. There is nothing to "solve" and no reward stapled to it — Wardffan beneath is ordinary (if fine) treasure, unconnected to the tears.

## MYSTERIES (no predetermined solution)
- **Why the dead won't stay drowned.** The flood-dead rose as ghouls; no curse, no necromancer is keyed. Maybe the foul water, maybe old battle-dead, maybe nothing nameable. Unresolved.
- **The weeping effigy.** See above. Never confirmed.
- **Why the hobgoblins feared below but squatted above the same dead.** Superstition and lost warriors, or something they saw? Leave it.
- **Whose servants the bricked toll-take really belonged to** — crown, the vanished garrison, or the march. A legal tangle, not a treasure map.

## DM NOTES
- **The place does not care about the party.** It guarded a border that no longer exists; it is indifferent. No plot, no clock, no villain with a plan — a warband squatting for plunder, dead that won't drown, and a merchant who just wants to go home.
- **No right way through.** Gate, climb the cracked NE tower, wade the river-breach into the undercroft, take the cistern drain, or parley/ransom with Grorm. Sneak, fight, bargain, or turn the hobgoblins against the dead below. All legitimate; no intended route.
- **HP is pre-rolled — use the numbers above, don't roll again, don't spawn extra.** When a room's creatures are engaged, instantiate them with these exact hit points for the attack tool. Reveal only what the PCs perceive.
- **The strange thing stays strange.** Whatever the table decides the weeping effigy is, the module never confirms it, and no monster or relic is hidden in it.
- **Treasure is here because it was lost, hidden, stored, or forgotten:** a bricked-up toll-take, raid-loot in the chief's chest, a deserter's flagstone cache, an old castellan buried with his blade. None was placed for heroes. Modest by design; Wardffan and the grave-silver are the fattest prizes, and they sit in cold water with the dead.
- **Scale to taste.** Shift ghouls between 11 and 12; the undercroft is the live heart of the place now that the surface is yours.
