# THE CROOKSPIRE

*A light old-school tower-keep module for AD&D 1e / OSRIC. Region: the march of Halvedd, the Known World (Orruvane), on the Wendwater ford-road. Nearest town: Wend, ~3 hours' walk north. Recommended: a solo 1st-level character (or 1-3 of levels 1-2) who knows when to withdraw. ⚠ DM-ONLY — do not read if you intend to play.*

---

## PREMISE
The Crookspire was a road-warden's watchtower — four floors of grey stone raised to watch the Wendwater ford, take a small toll off the drovers, and light a beacon that could be seen from Wend if raiders crossed. A warden and two men kept it. About thirty years ago the border quieted, the watch was disbanded, the last old warden died of a winter cough, and a lightning-strike cracked the top so the whole spire took a slow lean — "the crookspire," the carters called it, and stopped paying the toll. It was stripped of anything obvious and left to the weather. Now a clutch of kobolds dens in its cellar and ground floor, two stirges roost in the cracked top, and one harmless old beggar shelters on the warden's floor. On foggy nights the dead beacon still lights itself, cold and blue, and nobody knows why.

## THE GROUPS
**The Clutch (kobolds).** Five kobolds denning in the cellar and watching the ground floor — scavengers, no plan, terrified of anything bigger than themselves. Led by **Skritt**, who holds rank by having the only real knife. They steal from the carters' road and squabble. **Goal:** food, shiny things, not being killed. Morale breaks fast (they flee a Sleep or a downed comrade).
> **Skritt (kobold "chief"):** AC 7, HD 1, **hp 4**, hand-axe 1d6, morale 7.
> **Kobolds (4):** AC 7, HD 1-1, **hp 4, 3, 1, 1**, spear/dagger 1d4, morale 6.

**The roost (stirges).** Two stirges nesting in the cracked crown, drawn by the warmth that isn't there. They dart, stab, and latch on to drain blood — genuinely deadly to a 1-hp magic-user. This is the module's "withdraw and live" lesson: a fragile caster should not trade blows with them on a collapsing floor. **Goal:** blood; otherwise they ignore the tower.
> **Stirges (2):** AC 8, HD 1+1, **hp 9, 4**, attack 1d3 then attach & drain 1d4/round, fly 180 ft, morale 9 while feeding.

**Mip.** A half-deaf old beggar, ~70, who sleeps on the warden's floor under a heap of sacking and begs the kobolds' leavings. Non-combatant, harmless, frightened of the crown ("the blue light don't warm you, and it watches"). **The module's lore source:** remembers the warden, the toll, that the warden "kept his good things up his sleeve, behind the loose stone," and that the kobolds "won't go up past my floor — the buzzing things up top." Treat him gently and the tower opens up.
> **Mip:** non-combatant, AC 10, **hp 2**, no weapon.

## ROOM KEY

### 1. GROUND FLOOR — GUARDROOM
**Was:** the warden's guardroom and toll-post — a barred door to the road, arrow-slits, a bench, the toll-chest (long gone).
**Now:** the kobolds' front room. The road-door hangs on one hinge; daylight and wind come through the slits. Usually **1-2 kobolds** loiter here (from the Clutch above) unless drawn below. A **loose flagstone** under the old bench (search) hides **12 gp and 8 sp** — a toll-take the warden pocketed and never spent. The trap-stair down to the cellar (2) is in the floor; the main stair winds up to the warden's floor (3).

### 2. CELLAR — THE KOBOLD DEN
**Was:** the tower's cold-store and water-cistern.
**Now:** the Clutch's filthy den — bedding, gnawed bones, a banked dung-fire, the reek of kobold. **Skritt** and the rest of the kobolds are here when not on the ground floor; **3 giant rats** the kobolds half-tolerate skitter the edges.
> **Giant rats (3):** AC 7, HD 1d4, **hp 2, 4, 4**, bite 1d3 (5% disease), morale 5.
In a hole under Skritt's bedding, the clutch-hoard: **70 sp, 22 gp, a copper ring (2 gp), and a tarnished silver warden's whistle (10 gp)** that still shrills loud — blow it and every kobold in the tower bolts for the cellar (useful or disastrous). A side niche holds the old cistern, half-full of black water (foul).

### 3. WARDEN'S FLOOR
**Was:** the warden's quarters — bed, chest, a small writing-shelf for the toll-rolls.
**Now:** stripped to the walls and roofed by **Mip's** nest of sacking; he lives here. The kobolds leave him be (they won't pass him to the buzzing things above). Behind a **loose stone** in the chimney-breast (Mip will point to it for a kindness, or a search finds it) the warden's private cache: **30 gp, a garnet (50 gp), and a bone scroll-tube holding a Magic-User scroll of SHIELD** (1st level) — dry and good. The stair continues up to the store (4).

### 4. STORE / OLD ARMOURY
**Was:** the tower's dry-store and weapon-rack — spare spears, oil for the beacon, signal-flags.
**Now:** rot and ruin: a heap of perished flags, snapped spear-hafts, three sealed clay jars of **lamp-oil still good** (beacon oil — flammable, useful), and a **rat-nest** in the corner (no rats now; they moved to the warm cellar). A cracked step and a draught warn that the floor above (5) is failing. Nothing else of worth — but the oil and a thrown flame matter against the stirges or for light.

### 5. THE CROWN — BEACON PLATFORM  *(the one strange thing — do not explain)*
**Was:** the open signal-platform with its iron beacon-brazier, where the warden lit the warning-fire.
**Now:** the lightning-cracked, leaning top, open to the sky on one side where the wall fell. The **floor is unsafe** — anyone moving across it rolls to avoid a give-way: a missed save means a 30-ft fall to the store (4) for **2d6**, or clinging at the broken edge. **Two stirges** roost in the rafters here. And the strange thing: the old iron **brazier kindles itself** on foggy nights with a **cold blue flame** that gives no heat, casts no true light, and burns nothing — it has done so, on and off, since the warden died. *What it is, the module does not say.* There is nothing in the fire and no reward for staring into it; a character who does may feel briefly, wordlessly watched. Leave it unexplained.

## DM NOTES
- **Light and survivable for a careful solo 1st-level caster.** One Sleep folds the whole Clutch; the kobolds break the moment it goes off or the chief drops. The lesson-teeth are the **stirges + the failing crown** — a fragile magic-user who presses into the top instead of withdrawing can die there, and should. Reward the player who takes the coin, the scroll, and Mip's goodwill and leaves the buzzing dark above alone.
- **The place doesn't care about the party.** A warden's tower gone to kobolds and weather; no plot, no clock. If ignored, the kobolds keep filching the road and the crown keeps leaning until it falls on its own.
- **The strange thing stays strange.** The cold beacon is never explained and hides nothing. Resist stapling a monster or relic to it.
- HP is pre-rolled above — use those numbers; instantiate each room's creatures as you reach them and reveal only what the character perceives.
- **Suggested entry hook / placement:** the Crookspire stands on the Wendwater ford-road about 3 hours south of Wend (hex ~10,12). Alric can hear the rumors at the Broken Wheel and walk there in an afternoon.
