# DM KEY — HARROWGATE KEEP  ⚠️ DM-ONLY / SPOILERS
Campaign: The Known World. Hex ~ col 14, row 13 (ridge/hills above a river ford, ~a day+ E/SE of Wend on the old east road).
Style: B1/B2. A real place first. No plot/chosen-one/puzzle chains. ONE strange feature (the founder's blade). Keyed for **4th level, 1–3 PCs**. The keep predates and outlasts the party.

## 1. WHAT IT WAS
A royal toll-and-watch keep on the old march border: a small castle guarding a river ford and the east road. Garrison of ~30, collected tolls, watched the frontier, stored grain & arms, stabled patrol horses, kept a castellan's crypt.

## 2. WHY IT FELL
~60 yrs ago the border was redrawn by treaty; the keep lost its purpose and budget and the garrison marched out, sealing the last toll-take in the walls. A later spring flood undercut and BREACHED the river-wall, drowning the lower cistern/undercroft. Squatters drifted through; then worse moved in.

## 3. WHO'S THERE NOW (factions — 4th-level scale)
A) THE HOBGOBLIN FANG — a disciplined warband holding the gatehouse, bailey, and keep. ~14 hobgoblins (HD1+1, ~5 hp, AC5, longsword/morningstar 1d8, morale 8) + 3 ELITE GUARDS (HD2, ~10 hp, AC4) + SUB-CHIEF GRORM (HD3, 16 hp, AC4, two-hand sword 1d10, +1 dmg, morale 9). They drill in the bailey, fly a war-banner, post sentries, raid the east road and outlying steadings, take captives for ransom/labor. WON'T descend below the waterline (lost warriors to "the drowned dead"). Goal: dominate the road, grow the fang, eye Wend's outlying farms. Fang morale shaken if Grorm falls.
B) THE DROWNED DEAD — 5 GHOULS (HD2, ~9 hp, AC6, claw/claw/bite + paralysis save) in the flooded undercroft & castellan's crypt — the flood-/war-dead, risen. They incidentally guard the founder's tomb. (NOTE: ELVES are immune to ghoul paralysis — rewards an elf PC like Sylas.) The hobgoblins fear them and keep above.
C) THE CAPTIVES — a Wend wool-merchant, MASTER OREN PELK, + 2 servants, barred in the old guardroom (rm6) awaiting ransom. Oren knows the road, will pay/reward rescue, and (loose thread) his house-mark may be the same one from the Greywether dig tally if you want to tie threads. Info source + moral choice.

## 4. MYSTERIES (no fixed answer)
- Where's the sealed TOLL-TREASURY? Bricked into the wall of the gatehouse strongroom (rm1); a careful search / the castellan's seal (crypt) hints it. The hobgoblins haven't found it.
- What's in the flooded cistern? The ghouls/the drowned. Leave the "why they rose" unanswered — flood + old war-dead; NOT a curse-plot.
- Why won't the hobgoblins go below? They've lost warriors to the ghouls; superstition did the rest.

## 5. THE ONE STRANGE FEATURE
WARDFFAN — a +1 longsword interred with the FIRST CASTELLAN in the founder's tomb (crypt, rm12). Plain soldier's steel, faintly warm to an honest grip; +1 to hit & damage. Ghoul-guarded. That is the only magic in the keep.

## 6. TREASURE (4th-level scale; ~3,000–5,000 gp + magic)
- Grorm's warchest (rm4, lord's chamber): 1,200 gp, 600 sp, 4 gems (100,100,250,500 gp), a captured POTION OF HEALING and a SCROLL (Magic-User: Web + Mirror Image) taken in a raid, the war-banner (trophy).
- Sealed toll-treasury (rm1, behind bricked wall): an iron strongbox — 900 gp, 300 sp, 2 gems (250 gp ea), the castellan's silver seal-matrix (60 gp), old toll-rolls (worthless coin, useful info).
- Founder's crypt (rm12): WARDFFAN (+1 sword), grave-silver (a torc 200 gp, 2 arm-rings 75 ea), but ghoul-guarded.
- Granary tower cache (rm14): a deserter's old stash — 150 gp, a POTION OF CLIMBING, hidden under a flagstone; giant rats infest the tower.
- Oren Pelk (rescued): pays 500 gp on safe return to Wend + standing goodwill (a merchant contact/fence).

## 7. ROOMS (key)
SURFACE (curtain wall, square bailey, keep at NE):
1. GATEHOUSE & BARBICAN — 2 hobgoblin sentries, working rusty PORTCULLIS, murder-holes. Off the guard-passage: the STRONGROOM with the bricked-up TOLL-TREASURY (search/loose mortar).
2. BAILEY (courtyard) — drill-ground, cook-fires, banner-pole, a well. The fang's daytime life; usually 3–5 hobgoblins about. Open, watched from the wall-walk.
3. GREAT HALL / BARRACKS (keep ground) — hobgoblin common quarters: trophy wall, long hearth, weapon-racks. The bulk of the fang sleeps/eats here (5–8 present off-watch).
4. CASTELLAN'S CHAMBER (keep upper) — GRORM's quarters: furs, the WARCHEST, war-banner, a guard or two. Stair from the great hall.
5. KITCHENS & STORES (keep, behind hall) — food, a cook-crew (1–2), back stair down to cellars (10).
6. CAPTIVES' CELL (old guardroom, barred) — OREN PELK + 2 servants; 1 hobgoblin warder with the key. Near the gatehouse.
7. STABLES & FORGE (bailey lean-to) — raid-horses, a captured smith's tools/iron; 1–2 hobgoblins.
8. SOLDIERS' SHRINE (small, off bailey) — looted altar to a soldier-saint; hidden offering-niche (40 gp silver); stair DOWN to the crypt (12) — the hobgoblins avoid this stair.
9. WALL-WALK & TOWERS — sentry posts (1 sentry/side by day, fewer at night). The NE corner tower is FLOOD-CRUMBLED — a climbable breach into the bailey (alternate entry).
UNDERCROFT / FLOODED:
10. CELLARS & CISTERN-HEAD — partly flooded stores; the river-wall BREACH (rm15) lets water and intruders in; damp, dark.
11. FLOODED UNDERCROFT — knee-to-waist water, floating debris, drowned rooms; GHOUL hunting ground.
12. CASTELLAN'S CRYPT — burial niches, ossuary, the FOUNDER'S TOMB (WARDFFAN + grave-silver); the 5 GHOULS den here; source of the "drowned dead" the hobgoblins fear.
13. SALLY-PORT / CISTERN DRAIN — slimed tunnel with a short submerged stretch out to the riverbank: alternate entry/escape.
14. GRANARY TOWER (freestanding, bailey) — rotted grain, GIANT RATS; deserter's hidden cache under a flagstone (rm6 treasure list).
15. RIVER-WALL BREACH (exterior, riverside) — flood-broken wall; wade/climb into rm10/11, bypassing the gate entirely.

## 8. APPROACHES (non-linear)
Main gate/portcullis (hobgoblin-held) • climb the crumbled NE tower (9) into the bailey • the river-wall breach (15→10/11) into the flooded undercroft (ghouls, but bypasses the fang) • the cistern drain (13) from the river • the shrine stair (8→12) to the crypt • parley/bargain (Grorm may treat, ransom Oren, or be turned against the ghouls) • free & arm the captives as allies.

## 9. RUMORS (Wend; true/false/half)
1. "Hobgoblins have taken old Harrowgate and are raiding the east road — a steading burned last week." (TRUE)
2. "A Wend wool-merchant went missing on the east road a fortnight back." (TRUE: Oren, captive)
3. "When the garrison marched out, they sealed the last toll-take in the walls; never recovered." (TRUE)
4. "Men who camp near Harrowgate hear the dead walking the flooded cellars — even the hobgoblins won't go down." (TRUE)
5. "The first castellan was buried in the keep with his sword." (TRUE: Wardffan)
6. "The place is cursed and the hobgoblins are its thralls." (FALSE: just a warband squatting a ruin)

## 10. WANDERING (1d6; ~1/3 turns or per watch)
1) 1d6 hobgoblin patrol • 2) hobgoblin hunting party (1d4) returning w/ loot or a captive • 3) lone hobgoblin scout/sentry • 4) 1d3 ghouls (only below waterline or near crypt stair at night) • 5) giant rats (granary/cellars) • 6) eerie nothing: dripping water, a far horn, a slamming shutter.

## 11. DM NOTES
Present situations, not stories. The fang drills and raids on its own schedule; the ghouls hunt the wet dark; Oren waits. If the PC ignores the keep, it persists: the fang grows and raids Wend's farms; the ghouls stay penned below; Oren is ransomed or dies. Roll surprise/reaction/morale honestly. Hobgoblins are disciplined (good morale, organized response — a raised alarm brings numbers; reward stealth and picking them apart). Elves are immune to ghoul paralysis. Only add_npc / map a room when the PC actually meets/enters it.
